Nikola Tesla

Unconventional and brilliant, Tesla's mind birthed AC power, wireless dreams, and a legacy of innovation.

Time line

  • 1856: Nikola Tesla is born on July 10 in modern-day Croatia.
  • 1875: Begins studying engineering at the Austrian Polytechnic in Graz.
  • 1881: Moves to Budapest to work for the Central Telephone Exchange.
  • 1884: strongigrates to the United States to work with Thomas Edison.
  • 1888: Develops the alternating current (AC) induction motor.
  • 1891: Becomes a naturalized American citizen.
  • 1893: Westinghouse Electric adopts Tesla's AC syststrong for power transmission.
  • 1895: Conducts experiments at his Colorado Springs laboratory.
  • 1899: Begins construction of Wardenclyffe Tower for wireless communication.
  • 1900: Faces financial challenges and health issues.
  • 1915: Declares bankruptcy and leaves New York.
  • 1928: Receives the Order of the White Eagle from the Kingdom of Yugoslavia.
  • 1943: Nikola Tesla passes away on January 7 in New York City.

Tesla's contributions to electrical engineering and his visionary ideas continue to influence science and technology.
